## Purpose

Forward Deployed Engineers (FDEs) are becoming an increasingly common feature of the AI ecosystem, particularly amongst hyperscalers, AI platform providers and product companies. As the term becomes more widely used, it is important that CGI adopts a consistent interpretation and avoids applying it to roles that do not align with the established industry model.

This guidance explains what a Forward Deployed Engineer is, how the role differs from other consulting and engineering positions, where CGI has an opportunity to develop similar capabilities, and the considerations that should shape our own approach.

## Executive summary

A Forward Deployed Engineer is not simply an experienced engineer working at a client site. The role is fundamentally product-centric. An FDE combines deep expertise in a specific product with close engagement with business stakeholders, enabling rapid delivery of measurable business value while continuously feeding operational learning back into the product organisation.

CGI also has an opportunity to adopt a broader forward deployed delivery model that applies many of the same principles without being tied to a single product. While these two concepts share similar behaviours, they should not be treated as interchangeable. Maintaining this distinction will help us position our capabilities consistently with clients, alliance partners and the wider market.

## Market demand

### Current position

Explicit demand for Forward Deployed Engineers remains relatively limited. Most clients are not yet asking for FDEs by name. Instead, organisations are increasingly looking for engineers who can work alongside business users, understand operational problems quickly, build working solutions in short timescales and demonstrate tangible value before larger transformation programmes begin.

Demand is therefore emerging through the characteristics of the role rather than its title. This is particularly evident in AI adoption, product deployment, rapid modernisation initiatives and alliance-led engagements, where organisations want to accelerate value realisation from existing technology investments.

As AI platforms mature and clients seek faster returns on investment, demand for this style of delivery is expected to increase significantly.

## What is a Forward Deployed Engineer?

A Forward Deployed Engineer is a specialist who combines deep technical expertise with close customer engagement. Unlike traditional consultants or delivery engineers, an FDE is normally associated with a specific product or platform. Their expertise allows them to configure, extend and deploy that product rapidly without spending significant time evaluating technology choices or designing new architectures from first principles.

The role originated within product companies such as Palantir, where engineers work directly with customers to solve business problems while simultaneously improving the underlying product through continuous feedback. Product knowledge, customer proximity and rapid value delivery are therefore all essential characteristics of the role.

This differs from a more general forward deployed delivery model, where engineers embed with a customer to solve business problems but are free to select whichever technologies are most appropriate. Although both approaches emphasise speed, collaboration and measurable outcomes, only the former aligns with the widely recognised industry definition of a Forward Deployed Engineer.

## Characteristics of an effective FDE

Successful Forward Deployed Engineers combine deep technical capability with strong commercial awareness. They are motivated by business outcomes rather than technology for its own sake and are comfortable working directly with customers to understand operational challenges, shape solutions and iterate rapidly.

They thrive in environments where requirements are ambiguous, where priorities evolve quickly and where rapid feedback from users is expected. Rather than waiting for complete specifications, they create clarity through experimentation and close collaboration with stakeholders.

Perhaps most importantly, they remain focused on measurable business impact. Whether improving customer experience, reducing operational costs or accelerating service delivery, technology is viewed as a means to an end rather than the objective itself.

## Building capability

Developing this capability requires more than recruiting technically strong engineers. Forward deployed practitioners need a combination of engineering expertise, consulting skills, commercial awareness and the confidence to engage directly with senior client stakeholders.

CGI is already expanding capability through targeted recruitment, internal mobility, cross-skilling programmes, alliance-led training and AI bootcamps. These initiatives are creating a pipeline of engineers who can combine strong technical foundations with customer-facing delivery skills.

Demand for these skills is likely to outpace supply in the short term. Business units should therefore engage the Emerging Technology Practice early when opportunities requiring this capability begin to emerge.

## Future direction

As AI platforms become embedded within enterprise technology landscapes, organisations will increasingly seek partners who can demonstrate value rapidly rather than simply deliver technology. This represents a significant opportunity for CGI.

Our strongest opportunity may lie in combining our existing delivery strengths with a forward deployed operating model that accelerates adoption of AI platforms, modernises legacy estates incrementally and helps clients realise measurable value from existing investments.

Where the engagement is centred on a defined product, the industry term Forward Deployed Engineer should be used. Where the approach is broader and product-agnostic, CGI should describe the capability as a forward deployed delivery model, ensuring that the distinction remains clear to both clients and alliance partners.
