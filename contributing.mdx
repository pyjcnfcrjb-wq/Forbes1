This knowledge base is a community-driven project, and its quality depends on the collective effort of curious, knowledgeable readers. No single author can cover every subject with equal depth, which is why contributions from people with diverse expertise and perspectives make the resource stronger for everyone. Whether you've spotted a factual error, have a topic you'd love to see covered, or want to expand a stub article with your own knowledge, your input is genuinely valued and encouraged.

## Quality Guidelines

All contributions — whether a single correction or a full article — are held to the same standards that govern existing content. Before you submit, make sure your contribution meets the following criteria:

- **Accuracy** — Every factual claim should be verifiable. Where possible, support statements with references to credible, up-to-date sources such as peer-reviewed research, official documentation, or authoritative institutions.
- **Neutrality** — Articles aim to present topics objectively, especially where multiple perspectives exist. Avoid advocacy, promotional language, or framing that favors one viewpoint without acknowledging others.
- **Clarity** — Write in plain, accessible language. Define technical terms when you introduce them, use short sentences where possible, and organize your content with clear headings and structure. Aim to be understood by a reader with no prior background in the topic.
- **Citing Sources** — Include references for statistics, scientific claims, historical facts, and any information that is not common knowledge. Inline citations or a short reference list at the end of your contribution are both acceptable.

<Warning>
  Do not submit content that is copied or adapted from copyrighted sources without explicit permission. This includes text from textbooks, journal articles, websites, or other publications. All contributions must be original writing. Submissions found to contain copyrighted material will be rejected and, in repeated cases, may result in your account being restricted from future contributions.
</Warning>

## The Review Process

Every submission goes through an editorial review before it is published or merged into an existing article. Here's what you can expect:

1. **Initial screening** — Your submission is checked for obvious issues such as spam, off-topic content, or guideline violations. This typically happens within a few business days of submission.
2. **Editorial review** — A topic editor with relevant expertise reads your contribution for accuracy, clarity, and neutrality. They may leave comments requesting changes or clarifications.
3. **Revision (if needed)** — If edits are requested, you'll receive a notification with specific feedback. You'll have the opportunity to revise and resubmit before a final decision is made.
4. **Publication** — Once approved, your contribution is incorporated into the knowledge base and attributed appropriately. You'll receive a confirmation when your work goes live.

The review timeline varies depending on the complexity of the submission and the current volume of contributions, but most straightforward corrections and additions are resolved within two weeks.
