This knowledge base is structured to make information as easy to find as possible, whether you have a specific question in mind or you're browsing to satisfy your curiosity. Articles are organized into topic categories, cross-linked to related content, and written so that each page stands on its own while fitting into the broader picture. The guide below walks you through the best ways to get started and make the most of every visit.

## Getting Started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse by Category Using the Sidebar">
    The left-hand sidebar lists all major topic areas — Technology, Science, Society & Culture, Health & Wellness, and Reference. Expand any category to see its subtopics and individual articles. This is the best approach when you want to explore a subject broadly or aren't sure exactly what you're looking for yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the Search Bar for Specific Topics">
    If you have a particular question or keyword in mind, use the **search bar** at the top of the page. Type any term, concept, or phrase and you'll see matching articles ranked by relevance. Search works across all categories simultaneously, so you don't need to know which section an article lives in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow Cross-Links Between Related Articles">
    Throughout each article you'll find **inline links** to related pages. These connections help you follow a thread of curiosity naturally — for example, reading about nutrition might lead you to an article on metabolism, which in turn links to relevant biology concepts. Follow these links whenever you want more depth or context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the Reference Tab for the Glossary and Further Reading">
    The **Reference** section contains a glossary of key terms used throughout the knowledge base, curated reading lists for each major topic, and a FAQ page covering common questions. If you encounter an unfamiliar term or want to go deeper than a single article, the Reference tab is the place to go.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading Articles Effectively

Every article in this knowledge base follows a consistent structure so you always know what to expect:

- **Overview** — Each page opens with a concise summary of the topic, giving you the core idea in a few sentences before diving into details.
- **Key Concepts** — The main body of the article breaks the topic down into clearly labeled sections, each covering one concept or subtopic at a time. You can read straight through or jump to the section most relevant to you.
- **Examples** — Where helpful, articles include real-world examples, comparisons, and analogies to make abstract ideas concrete and easier to remember.
- **Related Articles** — At the end of most pages, you'll find a short list of recommended next reads to keep your learning going in a focused direction.

You don't need to read every article from start to finish. If you're already familiar with the basics of a topic, feel free to scan the section headings and jump to the part that answers your question.

<Tip>
  Bookmark articles you find especially useful so you can return to them quickly. Most browsers let you save pages with **Ctrl+D** (Windows/Linux) or **Cmd+D** (Mac), and you can organize bookmarks into folders by subject for easy reference later.
</Tip>
