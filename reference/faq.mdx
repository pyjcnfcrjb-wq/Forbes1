This FAQ page addresses the questions readers most commonly ask about how this knowledge base works — from how it's organized and kept up to date, to how you can get involved by suggesting topics or contributing content. If you're new here, reading through these answers is a great way to get oriented and make the most of everything available to you.

---

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How is the content organized?">
    The knowledge base is divided into broad subject areas — including Technology, Science, Society & Culture, and Health & Wellness — each containing individual topic articles, overview guides, and deep-dive pieces. Within each subject area, articles are grouped by theme and linked to related content so you can follow a natural path of exploration. A Reference section (where you are now) provides cross-cutting resources like this FAQ, a glossary, and further reading recommendations.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How often is the knowledge base updated?">
    Content is reviewed and updated on a rolling basis, with priority given to topics where new research, events, or developments make existing articles outdated. Major subject areas are audited at least quarterly, while articles covering rapidly evolving fields — such as artificial intelligence or public health — are reviewed more frequently. You'll find a "last reviewed" note at the bottom of individual articles where applicable.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is the information reviewed for accuracy?">
    Yes. Every article in the knowledge base goes through an editorial review process before publication. Factual claims are checked against authoritative sources — including peer-reviewed research, government agencies, and recognized expert organizations — and articles are revised whenever new information changes the established understanding of a topic. That said, no reference resource is infallible, and you should always consult primary sources or qualified professionals for high-stakes decisions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I suggest a new topic?">
    Absolutely — your input is welcome and genuinely shapes the direction of the knowledge base. If there's a subject you'd like to see covered that isn't currently included, you can submit a topic suggestion through the feedback form linked at the bottom of any article page. Popular or frequently requested topics are prioritized in the editorial calendar, and you'll receive a notification if and when your suggested topic is published.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What should I do if I find an error?">
    If you spot a factual error, an outdated statistic, or a broken link, please report it using the "Report an issue" link available on every article page. Include as much detail as you can — the specific claim in question, the article title, and ideally a source that contradicts or updates the information. The editorial team reviews all reports and aims to respond within a few business days.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I search for a specific topic?">
    You can use the search bar at the top of any page to search across the entire knowledge base by keyword, topic, or phrase. Search results are ranked by relevance and include a brief excerpt so you can quickly identify whether an article matches what you're looking for. If your search returns no results, try using broader or alternative terms — the glossary can also help you identify the standard terminology used in a given field.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use this knowledge base for academic research?">
    The knowledge base is designed as an educational starting point rather than a primary academic source. It is best used for building foundational understanding, identifying key concepts, and discovering authoritative resources to cite in your work. For formal academic research, you should follow up with peer-reviewed journals, government publications, or other primary sources — many of which are highlighted in the Further Reading section.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is this knowledge base available in other languages?">
    Currently, all content is published in English. Localization into other languages is a longer-term goal, and several high-priority articles are being prepared for translation. If you'd like to volunteer as a translator or see a specific language prioritized, you can indicate your interest through the feedback form. In the meantime, browser-based translation tools can provide a reasonable approximation for most articles.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I navigate between related topics?">
    Each article includes a "Related Topics" section at the bottom linking to connected articles within the knowledge base. You can also use the sidebar navigation to browse subject areas and discover neighboring topics you might not have thought to search for. Where a term used in one article is defined in another, it appears as an inline link so you can jump directly to the relevant explanation without losing your place.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the difference between the Knowledge Base and Reference tabs?">
    The Knowledge Base section contains the substantive articles on individual topics — explanations, histories, comparisons, and guides covering the breadth of each subject area. The Reference section, by contrast, contains supporting resources that span the whole knowledge base: the glossary, this FAQ, curated further reading lists, and other meta-level content. Think of the Knowledge Base as the library's books and the Reference section as the library's catalog and index.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are the articles suitable for all ages?">
    The knowledge base is written for a general adult audience, but the vast majority of content is appropriate for motivated readers of high school age and above. Articles avoid graphic content and are written to be inclusive and respectful across backgrounds and perspectives. Some articles covering health, history, or social issues may discuss sensitive topics in an educational context — these are handled carefully and factually, without gratuitous detail.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I contribute an article or section?">
    If you have subject-matter expertise and would like to contribute an article, you can apply to join the contributor network through the "Contribute" page linked in the site footer. Contributor submissions go through the same editorial review process as staff-written articles, and you'll be credited by name (or pseudonym, if you prefer) upon publication. The contributor guidelines document — also available on the Contribute page — outlines formatting expectations, citation requirements, and the editorial process from submission to publication.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
